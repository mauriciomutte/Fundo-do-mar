The physic and the mysteries of depths of the sea

When sitting in front of the ocean, have you ever wondered about how deep the ocean is and which mysteries does it hide?
Our planet's surface is 71% covered by water in the liquid state. 97,4% of that water comes from the oceans, resultingin around 68% of the planet being covered by the oceans' water. Over two thirds of our planet is covered by the oceans' water, yet 95% is still left unexplored by us, human beings.

Curiosities

Challenger Deep

The Challenger Deep is the deepest point of the ocean, more than 10,900 m below sea level. It is so deep that Mount Everest would fit there and it would still lack 2km to appear at sea level.

Exploration

The Challenger Deep was visited for the first time in 1960 by Jacques Piccard and Don Walsh. Since than, only one other person went down there, the cinema director James Cameron. The Challenger Deep was visited by only 3 people, while more than 10 astronauts walked on the Moon.

Mysterious sounds

The NOAA (National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration)'s undersea microphones have captured a list of mysterious sounds that resonate throughout the Pacific Ocean between late spring and early fall. To this day, it is not know what the origin of these sounds is.

Animals

Giant squid

Until 2013, giant squids with more than 1m were never observed in their natural habitat, 1000m deep. The giant squid was found in Japan, the animal was captured for analysis by researchers, but returned alive to the sea days later.

Anglerfish

The Anglerfish is one of the fishes that live in the depths of the ocean. He became famous for his appearance in the movie "Finding Nemo". The Anglerfish has some adaptations to live in the depths, like a kind of antenna in the head with light in the tip that is used to attract its prey.

Obstacles

Pression

Since water is much denser than air, every 10m below sea level, one more atmosphere of pressure is added. At 60m deep, a diver feels like seven atmospheres are squeezing it, enough for the air in the pressurized cylinder to become toxic.

Brightness

Between 200m and 1000m deep, sunlight completely dissipates into the ocean. For those who dive in this depth, the best flashlight doesn't illuminate more than 25m around them.
